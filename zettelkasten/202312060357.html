Variable Costs

Variable costs are costs that are directly proportional to how much a company sells their good or service.

Total Variable Cost = Total Quantity of Output x Variable Cost Per Unit of Output

In contrast to Fixed Costs, variable costs change throughout the lifecycle of a project. Since variable costs are directly related to the output of a business, they are never Indirect Costs but can be Direct Costs.

Examples

  • raw materials
  • direct labor
  • commissions
  • utilities
  • shipping/freight
