Lower-Self Motivations

We are not always perfect. Sometimes, our lower-self perks up with its motivations and grabs our attention. These motivations stem from a selfish, insecure, and petty place. They are an unhealthy, corrupt expression of our psychological needs [Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs].

Examples

  1. Seeking revenge for someone's wrong doing.
  2. Desire for validation from other people.
  3. Stroking your pride or ego.
  4. Desire to prove people wrong.
